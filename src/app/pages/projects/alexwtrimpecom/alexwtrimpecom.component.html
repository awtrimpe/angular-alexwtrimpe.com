In making this website, I never intended for it to be what it became, but it did turn out to be a pretty fun project that I really enjoyed and learned a lot from. I started making it because I had stupidly purchased a Raspberry Pi solely for the fact that I thought they were really cool seeing all of the projects people had done and how cheaply they can be purchased. So I found a really good deal on a set that came with the Pi, a case, an SD card, and the cord and then I made a late night purchase, because all great purchases that I make always seem to come right before the AM. It arrived really fast which is great except when the whole purchase was not a great buy anyway. I sat on it for a few months since I was about to graduate college, and then I got the idea to use it as a web server. I previously had a website but I was not fond of the idea of paying each month just to have a site to play around with, and this would be a really inexpensive way to go about hosting a site that I could have fun with and also learn a bit. The steps below are the steps I took in creating my site so you can copy it as well. I'm not going to lie, this was a lot more work than I thought it would be when I first started out. It would have been so much simpler, and admittedly better if I had just paid for a hosting service, but I learned so much in going this project and I continue to learn from it as I am constantly updating this site and trying new things. I would recommend it to anyone who is looking to learn something about hosting as well as just enjoy a project for yourself.

As mentioned, this all began from a Raspberry Pi, which you will also need if you want to replicate what I have done. Actually, you could use any other computer but the names of any of those machines are not nearly as cool as Raspberry Pi. If you do have a Raspberry Pi, it comes with an easy way to install an operating system. I chose Debian Stretch (9), and followed the instructions for that setup. I used Nginx as my server, but you can use Apache as well. This will lead you through my steps and hopefully this article will help you as well. If you are intending to just use this for a hobby site, I can absolutely get behind that and I will help you with achieving that dream. To start, I would recommend you plug in your Pi directly into an Ethernet port on the back of your router to ensure a constant connection. This may be slightly inconvenient, but it is much better in the long run. Next, you will need to either open up terminal on your Pi or SSH into it. You probably cannot SSH immediately but I would suggest this as it can be burdensome to log into your PI each time you want to update your site or change configurations of any kind. In the end of this article I will show you how to enable SSH. First, run the commands below after setting up your Pi in install your Nginx server.

After the service is running, Nginx should be serving a default page from the /var/www/html folder within your Pi. To view this default page, go to to IP address of the Pi. If you are unsure of the IP address the Pi has been assigned, log into your router and find the connected devices to your router. Most likely one is listed as Raspberry Pi and will a local IP such as: http://192.168.1.2. I wish I could give you more specific instructions about this, but every router is different and I do not what to lead you in the wrong direction. It is at this time that I would suggest you configure your router to always give the same IP address to your Pi. This will help in the next step and prevent you from having to reconfigure each time your router restarts.

It is next that you will want to open up a port on your router in order to forward public requests to your router's public IP, onward to your Raspberry Pi. If you intend to just host a regular website wtih no security certificates, just use port 80. If you want to use SSL (HTTPS) on your website, you will also need to open port 443. Foward both of these ports to the Pi. You can find these settings within the routing configuration rules on your personal router. Later, I explain that I use an API built in Python and Flask which I have running on an additional port. You will want to forward this port to your Pi as well if you want to make your website work with the same functionality I have. (By this port I mean whichever port you decide to run your API from. You can choose any but I normally do 5000).

Enable SSH

In order to update your website, you will want to have SSH enabled on your device. This will allow you to make changes to your website and update the files without having to transfer your files to a flash drive and then move manually to the Pi. This way, you can use an FTP client like WinSCP to transfer files to and from. In order to enable SSH, do the following steps:

  1. Run :sudo raspi-config
  2. Go to Advanced Options and SSH
  3. Select enable

That is all! You can now go off on your own project.

Making the Website

The reality is that you can use whatever you would like to make your own website. You can choose to right raw HTML or you can use a framework to build this out for you. No matter what it is, I challenge you to push yourself to learn something new. Make it in something you have never done before and make it in something you can get excited about learning. If you are using Nginx from the previous steps, I would avoid using anything with PHP though. It just does not work the best and may be a headache. In any regard, I used Angular and I encourage you to give Angular a go as well. It is a fairly well built package and can really help you get your app/project up and running in a sort time. To start, I would suggest checking out Angular's website for their suggestions on getting started. I wish I could provide some better guidance on getting your site up and running but every site is so unique that I may not be steering you in the right direction. Best of luck though and feel free to reach out if you have any questions!
