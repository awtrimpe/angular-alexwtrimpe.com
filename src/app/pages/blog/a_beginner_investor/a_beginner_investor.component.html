Before I go into anything financial, I want to explicitly state that investing involves risk and I am risking my own money. I am not accepting any monetary payments for this piece and all of these decisions are on my own accord from 1 year's experience in investing. I do not recommend you follow what I say to a T, but take what I have to say, keep from it what you will, and adapt your own methods. While I have been relatively successful in this instance, I am in no way guaranteeing your success. Above all, keep a watchful eye over your portfolio.

Now that is out of the way, I want to say that I am really excited about this topic. Anyone who knows me knows, I love talking stocks and the stock market. I am not a professional in the market and nor do I want to be. I just find it extremely interesting how the whole thing comes together and reacts to the world and sociological and economic changes. In the past year, I have really been exploring the new technology offered by Robinhood. If you have not heard of Robinhood, I really suggest you check it out. Robinhood offers completely free services for buying and selling stocks, options, and cryptocurrencies. They are continually expanding their services and offer this for no commissions or other fees. Since I know you are wondering, like I did, how Robinhood can make money, I will answer that for you. Just like a bank invests your money that you put into their establishment, Robinhood also invests the money that you have in your account that is sitting there not invested in anything (probably why they don't allow you to buy partial shares). They invest that bit of money which you aren't using, and that is how they make their returns. That might seem a bit shady, but seriously, who cares?? To pay no commission at all and trade on the fly, I will let them invest all of the money that I haven't. Your bank does it anyway so you already bought into that system. In any way, Robinhood is pretty cool and so far I fully back them. But since I am a new investor and fairly green into the world of investing, I wanted to offer my first-year advice for those who are also starting out so you don't have to repeat my same mistakes or you can learn from the lessons that I have learned.

When you read the advice given by older investors, they always say to look for the value. That means, look for a stock that is obviously undervalued given various conditions like current mismanagement, an apparent uptick in the specific market the business operates in, or other conditions that might make the value of that business increase. While that is definitely a good strategy and has proven effective, I think that the current shift in technology might allow for a new strategy that can pay off more quickly. The strategy I suggest is to find current "winners". A "winner" is a stock that is currently experiencing an uptick. This might be a recent rise (in the past month) an ongoing trend (past three months) or a long-term winner (this whole year has been up). For me, my winning range is a stock in the last year that has increased by at least 100% and is still doing well. These seem to be my bread and butter and I make some pretty quick and awesome money from them. My suggestion is to look at the trends of these stocks and follow the trend. Do not try to be the wise guy who has "found the bottom" because you never know when a stock is bottoming out. It will keep falling and you have no control over that. Be the one who doesn't let a trend completely pass them by and makes money on 3/4, 1/2 or even 1/4 of that upward trend.

A perfect example of what I am presenting is Warren Buffett and his purchase of Apple stock in 2016 and on. Since 2016, Warren has continually purchased more and more shares of Apple. Why would he do that? Because Apple is a winner! He doesn't need to predict future value, he knows that they are going to keep going up in value. Thus, why let the train pass you by completely when you can still take it to the end of the line instead of walking? This is what I am talking about. Find those stocks that are already doing well and hop on that train. It doesn't matter if you found the stock before anyone else did and bought it at its absolute lowest price. Sure that would make you cool, but it makes you stupid to kiss it goodbye forever because you are feeling down that you missed it at first. You are still saving time (aka making money), by hopping on the train!

Weibo

My personal example comes from the Chinese social media company Weibo. Weibo was trading for about $100 when I bought it. Within no more than two months, Weibo hit $140 and I was ecstatic! I had made my first quick buck and I thought about the possibilities on how high it could go. I mean, it had a hugely successful year and that history pointed to another great one (or so I thought). However, that was not the case at all. Weibo quickly fell to $135 and I took my money and ran. This is what I consider to be a perfect execution of my strategy. Ride a wave up, follow that wave to the top, and get off after you see the wave has crested. While it is very unlikely that you will be able to know when and how the crest is going to come, just look at it as getting out before it is too late. Just like finding when a stock bottoms out, you will never be able to adequately pick when the peak value is, so don't even try. You will just be kicking yourself over and over again trying to get it right.

My final tip and I cannot stress this enough, is DON'T PANIC. Man, have I really been upset when I sold something because it dipped 3% one morning just to watch it go all the way back up. I was so mad and I made that mistake multiple times. Sure I hopped back on and still made money with each buy and sell, but why not just hold it and not miss out on that whole upswing back to the top? Although I say not to panic, don't hold on to a bad thing. If something is dropping extremely fast (10% YIKES) or is consistently dropping day after day for a week, just let that little birdy free. You made you money and if it comes back, invite it back into your loving arms. But in the case like my first Weibo, when it doesn't come back, you look at that beauty of a trade with loving eyes forever.
