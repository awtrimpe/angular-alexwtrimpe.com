Moving To A New City After Graduation

  • By Alex Trimpe
  • June, 30 2018
  • 0 comments

There are few things in life that I looked forward to less than leaving my alma mater. I spent four years there doing what they said I should do, building my resume and learning so many valuable lessons. However, there was one saving grace to the fact that I was leaving all of my friends and comfort behind. That saving grace was the possibility to explore new things and learn about a new place that I would have to call home. After receiving a few job offers, I had the burdensome responsibility to find what place and organization I would let define the start of my professional career.

Look I want to start this off by being completely transparent, I am full of biases like everyone else, and my personal bias is that I think I am always right and I pick the best things. While that may not always be the case, I think I am right about one thing. That one thing is that I chose the perfect place for a recent college graduate to live, Detroit, MI. Strap in because I am going to enlighten you on one of the secret treasures of this great country. Back in the day, Detroit Michigan was the Silicon Valley of its era. Promising jobs, prosperity, and the excitement of living in close proximity to such great innovation, Since those golden days, Detroit has definitely seemed better of them. Sure it went through a pretty rough period, but here is the thing, it is not at all what you think it is now.

For people in the rest of the United States (where I am originally from), we all think that Detroit is reminiscent of some city that has barely survived a war and is now ridden with crime and lacking anything sustainable. What I found in this beautiful city is anything but. It is a place that is so vibrant and is trying so hard to be a haven that will attract new life and make it a great place to live. The thing that no one knows, IT IS!

"a place that is so vibrant and is trying so hard to be a haven that will attract new life"

You know the benefits of moving to a city that was once 1.8 million and is now 672,759? It is so wide open for possibilities. I'm not going to lie, there are definitely buildings which are abandoned and have been for quite some time, but there is also this beautiful side that has a reworked building or piece of art created right next to or out of that abandoned element. There are parks right along the river that are great for running and listening to concerts. There are trails in the city that were once railways but are now just beautiful paths with murals (See image above). There are downtown venues for great beer in the open and wonderful food that will help you get to the point where you can stay warm through the inevitably cold winter. You can throw a rock into Canada and you can find a great lake (haha get it?) in so many places nearby.

Best of all, it is so CHEAP! Name one other city that has rent for under $1,0000/month and is within walking distance to the main downtown. The food is exquisite, recycling is free, and there is even a rail line that runs downtown! That is unheard of with a population this low. All 4 major league sports teams are within walking distance and sight of each other and I am not sure there is even another city that has that possibility. I could keep going but here is the just of what I am trying to say.

What I also cannot understand is that rarely anyone in Michigan moves here. I have literally asked co-workers why they didn't move to the city and what they said was that it was "too expensive". Really? Because they then proceed to tell me that they pay more to live where they live 45 minutes out. That is crazy! I love the city and I think it is just going to get better. This city is gorgeous and absolutely wonderful. If you can endure a northern winter, and I happen to love the winter, this city in the summer definitely pays dividends.

As a recent college graduate who is looking for a great place to settle that has cheap living and amazing opportunities for young people, Detroit is absolutely your place. You can live in a place most young people want to live in but cannot afford, and you can stay busier than your schedule will allow. Take every opportunity to visit every place any local tells you to visit and make sure to explore the area. It is beautiful with all of the midwest hospitality included for no extra charge. Stop by the moment you get the change and may even stay a while!
